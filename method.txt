# Candidate Screening & Categorization Methodology

The system uses a sophisticated hybrid AI approach combining Deep Learning and Classical Machine Learning algorithms.

## 1. Resume Intake & Parsing
*   **Technique:** Regular Expression (Regex) Pattern Matching
*   **Method:** Extracts entities (Name, Email, Skills) from raw text.

## 2. Semantic Embedding (The "Language Brain")
*   **Algorithm:** **SBERT (Siamese BERT)**
*   **Model:** `all-MiniLM-L6-v2` (Transformer Neural Network)
*   **Function:** Converts variable-length resume text into a fixed 384-dimensional vector. This projection allows the system to calculate mathematical distances between resumes and job descriptions.

## 3. Automated Categorization (Clustering & Classification)
*   **Algorithm 1:** **K-Means Clustering**
    *   Used during the *Training Phase* to group the historical resume dataset into distinct "Profession Zones".
    *   Calculates the **Centroid** (geometric center) for each job category (e.g., the "average" Data Scientist vector).
*   **Algorithm 2:** **Cosine Similarity**
    *   Used during the *Inference Phase* (Live Screening).
    *   Calculates the cosine of the angle between a new candidate's vector and the K-Means Centroids. The candidate is assigned to the closest centroid (smallest angle).

## 4. Scoring Algorithm (Fusion)
*   **Technique:** **Weighted Linear Combination**
*   **Formula:** `Score = 100 * (w_S * Similarity + w_E * Exp_Norm + w_P * Proj_Norm + w_C * Cert_Norm)`
*   **Normalization:** **Min-Max Scaling** (via Scikit-learn) is used to scale structured data (Experience, Projects) to a 0-1 range.

## 5. Guidance & Recommendations
*   **Algorithm:** **k-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN)**
*   **Function:** Finds the *k* closest successful profiles (neighbors) to a lower-scoring candidate within the vector space.
*   **Application:** Identifying specific skills present in the "neighbors" but missing from the candidate (e.g., "70% of your nearest successful neighbors know Docker, but you don't").
